The divisor associated to a rational function \(f \in k(X)\) is

\begin{equation*} \divisor(f) = \sum_{p \in X} \ord_p(f) \cdot p. \end{equation*}

A divisor of this form is called a principal divisor. The group of all principal divisors is the group \(\Prin(X)\text{.}\) The Jacobian of \(X\) is the quotient \(\Jac(X) = \Div(X) / \Prin(X)\text{.}\)
